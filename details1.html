Francois Wahl

President, Numelec SA

A very distinguished nuclear physicist, Francois Wahl is the President of the Numelec SA (Geneva) Group. He was educated at the University of Orsay, France, where he graduated with a degree in physics and chemistry. He was also a graduate student at the University of Reading, United Kingdom, where he earned a Masters Degree in Business Management. He commenced his career as a Nuclear Engineer with Beecham from where he moved to establish Numelec SA, Geneva in 1977.

Francois was one of the founding members of the European Association for Magnetic Resonance Imaging in Medicine in 1981 and the European Bone Densitometry Organisation (EBDO) in 1993. To his credit, Numelec SA in 1981 installed the first MR Scanner in Europe. He also facilitated the installation Cyclotron, PET Scanner, Lasers and most high tech equipment in Europe in the 1980s. At that time, Numelec was solely responsible for the maintenance of these equipments.


Francois has constructed and rehabilitated several hospitals around the globe, especially in developing countries. An accomplished inventor, his patented inventions in the areas of Cone Beam Systems, Medical Appliances and Electric Cars have earned him gold medals at Geneva Invention Convention.

Francois sits on the Boards of EyeOptiCare SA Geneva, Leman SA Geneva and W/Car SA. He enjoys martial arts and is 1st Dan in judo and 5th Dan in Sumo. He is also the President of the Swiss Sumo Society and the Treasurer of European Sumo Union.

About Numelec

With its International Headquarters in Geneva, Switzerland, Numelec has a global procurement and construction operations network spanning over 90 Countries across 5 Continents.
